RULES & REGULATION

Rules and Regulations of the 929 Fire and Rescue Volunteers — created to maintain discipline, unity, respect, responsibility, and proper coordination within the group, ensuring professional and dedicated volunteer service to the community.

Core Articles

Article I – Participation

▼

Section 1

Members are expected to arrive on time for all meetings, deployments, and call‑outs to ensure smooth coordination, proper flow of activities, and the safety of everyone involved.

Section 2

Members must inform the officers ahead of time if they will be absent or running late so plans can be adjusted and responsibilities can be reassigned properly.

Section 3

Members are encouraged to take part actively in all training and official activities to strengthen teamwork of the group, improve skills, and maintain readiness during emergency situations.



Article II – Dress Code, Proper Gear and Equipments

▼

Section 1

The uniform of 929 must always be worn during operations, activities, and other group events. However, if the uniform is dirty or if a member does not have the group uniform, it is required to wear a black T-shirt, pants, and shoes to maintain a formal appearance.

Section 2

All members must purchase or acquire their official uniforms, and they are required to have a valid uniform for a duration of one year. If they do not possess one, it will be considered their first offense of violation.

Section 3

All members must purchase or acquire their official IDs of the group, and they are required to have a valid ID for a duration of 5 months. If they do not possess one, it will be considered their first offense of violation.

Section 4

Members are encouraged to use their uniforms for various activities outside the group, except for casual outings, social gatherings, drinking events, or showing off to others. It should be worn appropriately.

Section 5

Each member must have their own personal protective equipment (PPE) for various purposes. BORROWING IS PROHIBITED (Article 6). Members should not wear any equipment belonging to others, especially in public, unless permission has been granted.

Section 6

Group/Non-Group equipment responsible for ensuring each item is used with care, protected from damage, and maintained so it remains ready for operations or activity. Ensure that all personal and team equipment is well-maintained to guarantee proper functionality during emergencies and events.

Section 7

Return borrowed items after use to help keep everything organized, available, and in good condition for the next team member who may urgently need them.

Section 8

No unauthorized use of IDs, uniforms, radio frequency, or vehicles to maintain safety, protect the group’s identity, and prevent misuse that could affect ongoing work or operations.



Article III – Safety Protocols

▼

Section 1

Follow all safety procedures during every activity and operation to protect yourself and others at all times.

Section 2

Do not perform any task without clear instruction, proper guidance, or the presence of an authorized supervisor or ground commander.

Section 3

Report any safety concern or potential danger immediately so the proper steps can be taken.

Section 4

Entering in an area without proper protective equipment and training is strictly prohibited. The same on Article 5, Section 2 when giving first aid to someone with an external or internal fracture or wound. Always ask for permission before touching or helping the patient.



Article IV – Communication

▼

Section 1

GC is strictly for official announcements and coordination. It serves as the main space for sharing clear instructions, schedules, and important reminders to keep everyone aligned.

Section 2

Unrelated chats, spam, and unnecessary replies are discouraged to maintain order in the group. This helps ensure that important messages remain easy to find and understand.

Section 3

Prompt responses to roll calls and important updates are expected, as these messages help track readiness, availability, and immediate actions when needed.

Section 4

Members are not allowed to leave the official group chat without informing or securing permission from the officers or administration. Leaving the GC without valid reason may be considered a violation of group coordination and communication protocols.



Article V – Professionalism, Confidentiality and Discipline

▼

Section 1

Handle all information, photos, and reports with great care. Do not share anything outside the group without clear approval or permission.

Section 2

Protect the personal details of members and anyone involved in an incident to ensure their safety and dignity.

Section 3

Every member is expected to follow the group rules to keep duties and operations running smoothly.

Section 4

Show respect at all times, both in main GC (Group Chat) or other GC by organising the group and on-ground by treating every member with kindness and understanding.

Section 5

No offensive, rude, or unnecessary messages, allowing discussions to remain professional and welcoming.

Section 6

Follow the chain of command, supervisor and respect officer instructions so tasks are carried out smoothly, safely, and without confusion. The decisions of officers must be acknowledged and followed as a sign of respect for leadership.



Offenses & Disciplinary Actions

Any member or officer who violates any provision, section, or requirement stated in the Articles above shall be subject to disciplinary action. The group recognizes three (3) levels of offenses.


First Offense: Verbal/Written

  • Official letter warning (subpoena) issued by the 929 System Administration containing full facts and supporting evidence.
  • The member is required to submit a written explanation or verbal statement within fifteen (7) days.
  • Failure to respond within the given period shall automatically elevate the case to the Second Offense.

Second Offense: Investigation

  • Applies if the member remains unresponsive through social media or digital platforms to contact.
  • The member shall be added to an official disciplinary GC for formal evaluation.
  • If uncooperative, authorized officers may conduct an unannounced visitation to the member's residence.
  • If the member is unavailable, refuses to appear, or continues to avoid communication, the case shall proceed to the Third Offense.

Third Offense: Final Action

  • Applies if the member continuously ignores warnings, official letters, and residential visitations.
  • The Chief of Administrative Officer and the Group President shall determine the final penalty.
  • The highest penalty under this level is removal or expulsion from the group.

Severe Violations & Automatic Expulsion

Any member who commits major offenses that endanger the group's reputation, safety, or public standing will face immediate permanent expulsion without undergoing the standard three-offense warning system.

Physical assault or violence
Serious operational misconduct
Criminal activities and acts
Theft, robbery, or unlawful actions
Severe group reputation damage
Extreme misconduct / Dangerous behaviors

• Record Validity: All recorded offenses shall remain active for a period of one (1) year. A member must maintain a violation-free record for 1 solid year to completely reset or clear their previous offense history.

• Proposals: Members and officers are allowed to submit additional rules provided they contain a comprehensive explanation, purpose, facts, evidence, and target benefits for evaluation.
